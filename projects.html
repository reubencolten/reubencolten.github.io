Current Projects

  • Software Engineering: Fake Stack Overflow Project

    In my software engineering class, I’m collaborating with a small group using an agile development process to add new social features to an existing web app modeled after StackOverflow. We’re adding a new following feed to the app and expanding the user profile and user privacy options to enable greater personalization, as well as enhancing existing components like the question filtering system and the upvote/downvote system. The app uses a server/client architecture with a Typescript backend and a React frontend. Other dependencies include Express, axios, socket.io, and Mongoose for managing the data stored in the MongoDB database.

  • Data Visualization and Presentation: Video Game Sales Analysis Website

    I’m currently working in a small group to develop data visualizations that explore what features, such as genre, platform, and publisher, make a video game successful in different regional markets. We’re using data visualization libraries like Altair, D3, and Plotly to create both interactive and static visualizations, and our analysis and explanation of the story we’re presenting will be compiled on a project website.

Other Projects

  • FACES Literary Magazine, May 2023

    My senior year of high school I was co-editor of the school’s literary magazine, FACES. That Spring, I took the lead on compiling and designing layout of the visual art and writing based submissions in Adobe InDesign, as well as coordinating with the printing company about a new format and the printing timeline. That year we were able to achieve the long-desired goal of having the magazine printed and ready for distribution at the school’s Mayfest event in mid-May, allowing us to share the magazine more broadly than when it was distributed at graduation in the past.
